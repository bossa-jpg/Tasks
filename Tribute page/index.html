Hayao Miyazaki

"All I wanted to do was to make something beautiful"

Here's a time line of Hayao Miyazaki career:

  • In 1963 Miyazaki at Toei Animation worked as an in-between artist on the anime "Wan Wan Chuushingura", "Okami Shonen Ken", "Gulliver no Uchuu Ryokou" (1964).
  • 1968 - He worked as chief animator, concept artist, and scene designer on "The Great Adventure of Horus", "Prince of the Sun".
  • 1969-1970 - Under the pseudonym Saburō Akitsu, Miyazaki wrote and illustrated the manga "People of the Desert", published in 26 installments in "Shōnen shōjo shinbun".
  • 1971 - Miyazaki left Toei Animation. He was hired at A-Pro with Isao Takahata and directed 23 episodes of "Lupin the Third Part I".
  • In 1972 and 1973, Miyazaki wrote, designed and animated two "Panda! Go, Panda!"
  • In June 1973 Miyazaki and Takahata moving from A-Pro to Zuiyō Eizō and worked on "World Masterpiece Theater". Zuiyō Eizō continued as Nippon Animation in July 1975
  • 1978 - Miyazaki directed the television series "Future Boy Conan"
  • 1979 - Miyazaki left Nippon Animation and during the production of "Akage no Anne". He moved to TMS Entertainment, to direct his first feature anime film, "Lupin III: The Castle of Cagliostro"
  • In 1981 Miyazaki directed six episodes of "Meitantei Houmuzu"
  • In November 1982 Miyazaki resigned from Telecom Animation Film
  • The manga—titled "Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind"—ran from February 1982 to March 1994
  • 11 March 1984 "Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind" was released
  • In April 1984, Miyazaki opened his own office in Suginami Ward, naming it Nibariki
  • In June 1985, Miyazaki, Takahata, Tokuma and Suzuki founded the animation production company Studio Ghibli
  • 1986, Studio Ghibli's first film, "Laputa: Castle in the Sky"
  • "My Neighbor Totoro", was released in April 1988
  • In 1987, Studio Ghibli acquired the rights to create a film adaptation of Eiko Kadono's novel "Kiki's Delivery Service"
  • From March to May 1989, Miyazaki published the manga "Hikōtei Jidai". In 1992 by motive this manga realesed the film "Porco Rosso"
  • "Princess Mononoke" premiered on July 12, 1997. The film was commercially successful and becoming the highest-grossing film in Japan for several months.
  • The film "Spirited Away" was released on July 20, 2001; it received critical acclaim, and is considered among the greatest films of the 2000s.
  • "Howl's Moving Castle" was released on November 20, 2004
  • Miyazaki received the honorary Golden Lion for Lifetime Achievement award at the 62nd Venice International Film Festival in 2005.
  • Following "Ponyo" release on July 19, 2008, it was critically acclaimed, receiving Animation of the Year at the 32nd Japan Academy Prize.
  • The film "Kaze Tachinu" premiered on July 20, 2013, and received critical acclaim; it was named Animation of the Year at the 37th Japan Academy Prize, and was nominated for Best Animated Feature at the 86th Academy Awards.
  • In August 2016, Miyazaki proposed a new feature-length film, "How Do You Live?", on which he began animation work without receiving official approval. He hopes to complete the film by 2019, though Suzuki predicts a 2020–2021 release.
